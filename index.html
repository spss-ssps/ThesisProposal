thesis proposal

This project explores personal and ancestral memory through movement, archival media, and a wearable project. It investigates how traces of individual and inherited memory manifest in the body and rhythm.

In what ways can traces of personal and ancestral memory inhabit the body, and how can movement and technology make them perceptible and tangible?

Archival Website

This semester took a class called Medium of Memory with Simone Salvo where I started to explore these ideas.

I was interested in exploring how bodies and movement are imprinted over time. Showcasing it in this first project for the class:

Victoria Santa Cruz:
“rhythm is ‘the great organizer.’ It is key to our connection to the secrets of the organic cultures of the past. We can discover rhythm with the vehicle of the human body, never with the cold, colonizing intellect… the secret nature of rhythm, inherited from ancestral cultures, cannot be expressed through definitions (‘tempo,’ ‘beat,’ ‘meter,’ ‘movement,’ ‘order,’ etc.), for these definitions are only consequence of rhythm (just as dance is a consequence of rhythm).

Santa Cruz was a important figure in Afro-Peruvian culture and music.

As a final project I created this second website where I started to compile media and research on this topic. In the thesis iteration I am hoping it will function as a digital archive that organizes and visualizes collected media, movement studies, and research

Wearables

For the wearable component of my thesis, I want it to reflect echoes of movement and memory. I plan to construct the garment using crochet or a similar textile technique, allowing the layering of material to mirror the accumulation of archival traces. This explorationg started with my NIME class where I am created a wearable intrument.

Performance

As a dancer, I want this project to have a performance component. I am considering combining live projection mapping of archival media onto the body with real-time body tracking, potentially using MediaPipe (or some sort of sensor to be built-in to the wearable). The projections will respond to the dancer’s movements, creating a dialogue between the performer and historical material.

This is a touchdesigner and media pipe exploration, not necessarly to be a part of the peformance.
